About

Alexia Asgari is a creative technologist, innovation strategist, and computational designer whose work explores the evolving relationship between humanity and technology. Through interdisciplinary media, her art engages themes of craft, mechanization, and digitalization. She holds a Master of Design (Technology) from the Harvard Graduate School of Design and a Bachelor of Architecture with a minor in Perceptual Cognition/Neuroscience from Cornell University.

Artist Statement

My practice operates within liminal space: the productive ambiguity between humanity, craftswork, and technology. I investigate the tensions and convergence, control and chaos, as generative conditions for making.

Central to this inquiry is a persistent curiosity about humanity expressed through ostensibly non-human forms. Translating neurological responses into physical forms, examining the dialogue between humans and machines, questioning how digital systems categorize or alter human experience, and reimagining how we understand our bodies through sensory mapping: I tangibly and digitally render indexical traces of embodied experience. Preserving ephemeral psychological conditions.

My methodology embraces technological failure as expressive content. I approach digital systems not merely as neutral instruments, but as media carrying their own material logics and limitations: constraints that when foregrounded rather than suppressed, become sites of meaning. This practice of "breaking the code" transforms glitches, artifacts, and systemic boundaries into aesthetic and conceptual opportunities.

Throughout this work runs a sustained commitment to narrative. Each piece participates in an ongoing dialogue between maker and machine, asking how automated processes might accommodate human agency, and how technological mediation reshapes our understanding of authorship, control, and emotional connection.
